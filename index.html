Manifesto · அறிக்கை

Three Pillars

What I actually want to change — and how.

01
Professionalise

TSoc deserves proper infrastructure. Right now too much lives in people's heads or in old WhatsApp chats — that needs to change.

  • A real TSoc website

    One place for memberships, event tickets, and information — so members aren't hunting across five different group chats to find out what's on.

  • Actual payment systems

    No more bank transfers. Stripe-integrated ticketing means faster payments, automatic records, and far less admin stress for whoever's treasurer.

  • Slack for committee comms

    Separate channels for events, publicity, and sponsorships — so conversations are searchable, nothing gets buried, and new committee members can actually get up to speed.

  • Institutional memory

    Proper written handovers, contact lists, and event templates — so the next committee isn't starting from zero and we stop losing things when people graduate.

02
CPD & Events

Events with a point — not just nights out, but things people actually remember and come back for.

  • Careers events that feel useful

    Termly sessions with Tamil professionals in law, finance, medicine, and tech — speakers who actually look like us, talking about paths we might want to take.

  • More events like the civil war discussion

    Space to talk honestly about Tamil history and current affairs — with proper framing and context circulated beforehand so it's an informed conversation, not just noise.

  • Charity with context

    Every charity event should explain where the money is going and why it matters. Post-event updates so members can see the actual impact of what they gave.

  • Keeping older members in the picture

    Dinners, collaborative socials, networking events — a calendar that isn't just aimed at first-years, because TSoc should feel relevant through all three years.

03
Community & Continuity

A Tamil community that lasts beyond graduation and grows bigger than just Cambridge.

  • Alumni directory

    A searchable database so members can find each other for mentorship, job referrals, or just staying in touch — built properly so it doesn't die after one year.

  • Actual sponsorship pipeline

    Approach Tamil and South Asian-owned businesses with a proper pitch — clear value for event exposure, branding, and community reach, not just a cold email.

  • UK-wide Tamil student network

    Joint events with TSocs at Oxford, UCL, and beyond — so Cambridge isn't operating in isolation and we're part of something bigger.

  • Monthly newsletter

    A short, actually-worth-reading email — society news, Tamil current affairs, and a quick language or cultural bite. Something you open, not something you delete.
