Manifesto

Three Pillars

A focused agenda built on professionalisation, meaningful events, and long-term community continuity.

01
Professionalise

Transform TSoc into a professionally run society with clearer systems, stronger accountability, and continuity beyond each committee.

  • New TSoc Website

    I will build a central TSoc platform for memberships, event tickets, announcements, and useful society information, so members are not relying on scattered messages across different chats.

  • Seamless Digital Payments

    Use Stripe or Ticket Tailor for tickets and payments, making transactions faster, traceable, and easier to manage while reducing admin pressure on the committee.

  • Slack for Committee Comms

    Move committee communication onto Slack with dedicated channels for publicity, events, sponsorships, and handover — so discussions are organised and searchable.

  • Notion for Documentation

    Create a permanent Notion workspace for event templates, contacts, handover notes, and onboarding guides, so every committee inherits a proper system rather than restarting from scratch.

02
CPD & Events

Shift TSoc toward events with real value — intellectual, professional, and cultural.

  • CPD & Networking Sessions

    Run termly sessions with Tamil professionals in law, finance, tech, and other fields, with targeted outreach to alumni and external contacts to make the events genuinely useful.

  • Intellectual Heritage Events

    Host discussions on Tamil history and contemporary issues with proper framing and pre-circulated context, creating space for thoughtful and informed conversation.

  • Purpose-Driven Charity

    Every charity event will explain where the money is going and why it matters, with post-event updates so members can see the direct impact of what they supported.

  • Retention-Focused Events

    Plan events for more than just freshers — formal dinners, collaborative socials, and networking events designed to keep older members involved throughout the year.

03
Community & Continuity

Build a Tamil community that lasts beyond Cambridge and remains connected over time.

  • Alumni Directory

    Set up a searchable alumni database to support mentoring, referrals, and long-term engagement with TSoc after graduation.

  • Sponsorship Strategy

    Build a proper sponsorship pipeline targeting Tamil-owned businesses and relevant brands, with clear value propositions for event exposure, branding, and partnership opportunities.

  • UK-Wide Collaborations

    Work with Tamil societies across the UK to scale joint events and position Cambridge TSoc as part of a wider student network.

  • Monthly Newsletter

    Launch a professional monthly email brief with society updates, Tamil current affairs, and short cultural or language features to keep members engaged year-round.
