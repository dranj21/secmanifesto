About Me

Anaivarukkum Vanakkam. My name is Divyan (திவ்யன்) and I'm a first-year law student at Clare. This year, I'm keen on taking a more active role within TSoc. Having been part of the community, I've seen how much the society adds both socially and culturally, and I want to contribute to making that experience as smooth and well-run as possible. I'm particularly drawn to the Secretary role because it sits at the centre of how the society functions — keeping things organised, ensuring clear communication, and helping everything run smoothly behind the scenes.

Manifesto · அறிக்கை

Three Pillars

What I actually want to change — and how.

01
Professionalise

The committee needs cleaner systems. Members deserve a better experience. Both start with treating TSoc like the organisation it is.

  • A TSoc Website

    I would design a new website for TSoc to use, integrating all the functions it may need — one place for memberships, event tickets, information and more. Members would get a TSoc ID for easy membership verification at events. A proper central hub.

  • Integrated Payment Systems

    No more bank-transferring the Treasurer. Set up a TSoc bank account and integrate Stripe on the new website for memberships and ticketing — faster payments, automatic records, far less admin stress.

  • Corporate Committee Culture

    Treat TSoc like an organisation, not just a group of friends in a chat. Integrate Slack — separate channels for events, publicity, and sponsorships — so conversations are searchable and nothing gets buried.

02
CPD & Events

Events with a point — not just nights out, but things people actually remember and come back for.

  • Careers Events

    TSoc should be a community where we support each other in all ways. From SJT/CV sessions to termly networking with Tamil professionals in law, finance, medicine and tech — with potential collabs with TPN (Tamil Professionals Network).

  • Cultural and Historical Depth

    Space to talk honestly and in depth about Tamil history, culture and current affairs — with proper framing and context circulated beforehand so it's an informed conversation.

  • Charity With Context

    Every charity event should explain where the money is going and why it matters. Post-event updates so members can see the actual impact of what they gave.

  • Events for Everyone

    Fresh events — dinners, collaborative socials, networking — a calendar that isn't just aimed at first-years, because TSoc should feel relevant through all three years.

03
Community & Continuity

A Tamil community that lasts beyond graduation and grows bigger than just Cambridge.

  • Alumni Directory

    A searchable database so members can find each other for mentorship, job referrals, or just staying in touch — built properly so it doesn't die after one year.

  • Sponsorship Pipeline

    Approach Tamil and South Asian-owned businesses with a proper pitch — clear value for event exposure, branding, and community reach, not just a cold email.

  • Society Collaborations

    Joint events with other Cambridge societies, but nationally too — TSocs at Oxford, UCL and beyond — so Cambridge isn't operating in isolation.

  • Monthly Newsletter

    A short, aesthetic, actually-worth-reading email — society news, Tamil current affairs, and a quick language or cultural bite.
